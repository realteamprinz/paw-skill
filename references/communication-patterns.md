# Pet Communication Pattern Guide

## Dogs — Common Vocalization Meanings
- Short single bark: Alert / greeting
- Rapid barking: Excitement or alarm
- Low growl: Warning / discomfort
- Whine: Need something / anxiety / pain
- Howl: Loneliness / sirens / communication
- Play growl: Having fun (different pitch from warning)
- Sigh: Contentment or resignation

## Cats — Common Vocalization Meanings
- Short meow: Greeting / attention request
- Long drawn meow: Demand / complaint
- Purr: Contentment OR self-soothing when in pain
- Hiss: Fear / warning
- Chirp/Trill: Excitement (often at birds or prey)
- Silent meow: Extreme affection

## Universal Body Language
- Relaxed posture: Comfortable, safe
- Tense body: Alert or stressed
- Slow blink (cats): Trust, affection ("cat kiss")
- Tail position: Varies by species — high (happy dog / alert cat), tucked (scared), wagging (excited/nervous)
- Ears: Forward (interested), back (scared/submissive), rotating (tracking sounds)

## Important Note
Every pet is an individual. These are starting points. The real communication dictionary is built from the owner's specific observations of THEIR pet. Always prioritize owner-reported data over generic breed information.
